On Managing Uncertainty

“

     It's almost something I've forgotten - not uncertainty, but the decision to "handle" it, to wrest from it some control. I was so good at my job, in high school - at being a student - that I have an official transcript from Ball State University on which it is declared that I earned 64.000 (with 3 zeroes, for whatever reason) college credit hours with a 4.0 GPA. I was on the Dean's List as a Junior in High School. I took credit overloads during the year and college summer camp courses during the summer. I could have completed a 4 year degree in 2 years, had I gone to any state college in Indiana (or probably elsewhere). This year would have been my senior year. I may not even have needed to stay the whole year, if I'd paid for course overloads the year before. Some places would have paid me a stipend for living expenses so long as I stayed a student. I would be in complete control of my life, my grades, my work-study, everything. And I would have been miserable.

Incredibly strange, isn't it?

I hate flat design

“
I once said that the most difficult things to design are the simplest. For instance, to improve the form of a scalpel or a needle is extremely difficult, if not impossible. — Raymond Loewy
”

Raymond Loewy: in a sense, he defined industrial design. He was a genius; a man who, in his own words, sought to make "the daily life of the 20th century more beautiful." He succeeded. Among his famed designs is the coca-cola bottle, the lucky strike cigarette packaging, and a long, iconic line of after-war studebakers in the 1950s.

He believed in simplicity. He hated plastics and chrome - the design standards of the century, the materials that rapidly and inexorably changed the world. He believed in design that had body, that was simple, that was sleek. The Father of Streamlining, The Father of Industrial Design, The Man Who Shaped America. These are his appelations; his reward for modern obscurity, for excellence and subtlety.

“
More than function itself, simplicity is the deciding factor in the aesthetic equation. — Raymond Loewy
”

So what in hell is simpler than flat design?

Nothing. No design at all. I'm talking Mother Fucking Website design. WHY THE HELL. DO WE DESIGN. THINGS. SO. COMPLICATED.

I am text over an image. I am shadows and colors. I am described ENTIRELY in 100 103 lines of styles. I do not believe that style should take more space than content. And I already have less than 100 lines of content in this post.

"But Jordan, is not flat design quite simple?"

No. Flat design is a rave-siren taped on the breast of a Wordpress-powered, code-bloated beast. Now we not only must bear his ugliness, his bloated, horrid body; now we must pay attention to him. Now he is everywhere, blasting his pastels and long-shadows about like vomit.

Flat design is the 21st century parallel to Loewy's abhorred plastic. And it is anathema to simplicity.

I want shelves on an app - wait for it - to look like effing shelves. (I don't know why so many things are designed with shelves, but it seems quite prevalent.)

I want buttons - don't be surprised, now - to have bevels (gasp!), to have depth (oh gawd no), and to depress when I click them.

Perhaps worst of all?

I actually like gradients, pictures, and silly things like letters. I know. I know. Letters? Why the hell do we need letters when I can draw up a vector graphic of an iPhone with long shadows and buttons and animations in SVG that scale to twenty different screen sizes? Hell, Jordan â€” I can make it dance! Dance!

I'd rather you just type out "this is an iPhone app."

You can still have your fancy vector graphic with animations. Maybe you should ditch the long-shadow, since those are just gross, but you can have the picture. Just make sure you don't forget to tell me what the fuck it is. I don't care what color the background is or what version of iPhone you decided to draw - hell, I don't care if Steve Jobs himself blessed your graphic with his dying breath. Tell me when you show me. I'm tired of videos and iPhones and all that shit.

In other words: design should get to the point. Flatness is not simplicity. It's just a different way to brand complexity.
